Research Methodologies (PhD Projects)

1. Development of Hybrid Digital Twin for Structural Health Monitoring (SHM)

The first major PhD project involved developing a hybrid model combining advanced modeling techniques, RB space, and a data-driven approach using deep learning. The goal was to create an accurate and efficient DT framework for real-time SHM. This project focused on integrating:

  • Finite Element Analysis (FEA): FEA was employed to simulate structural behaviour under various loading conditions, serving as the foundational physical model for the RB space establishment.
  • Advanced Modelling Techniques: Model Reduction Techniques (MOR), particularly RB methods, were utilised to reduce the computational complexity of large-scale structural models, allowing for real-time simulation and faster decision-making.
  • Data-Driven Approach: A data-driven approach, primarily through deep learning, was incorporated to enhance the predictive capabilities of the DT.
  • Deep Learning for Real-Time Structural Damage Identification: Deep learning algorithms were integrated to provide advanced anomaly detection by learning from RB snapshots used as datasets for the network.

2. Development of a Cost-Effective IoT Solution for SHM

The second PhD project involved designing and implementing a cost-effective Printed Circuit Board (PCB) for IoT-based SHM.

  • Hardware Development: Focused on designing a low-cost, energy-efficient PCB to gather real-time structural data.
  • DT Synchronisation: Ensured that the physical data collected by the PCB through sensors could be integrated into the DT in real-time.
