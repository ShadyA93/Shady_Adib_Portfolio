About Me

I am Dr. Shady Adib, a PhD candidate at the University of Newcastle, UK. My research focuses on leveraging digital technology and data-driven approaches to innovate real-time damage identification in infrastructure.

Research

My PhD research focuses on the development of Digital Twins (DT) for monitoring and managing the health of civil engineering structures.

  • Development of real-time structural anomaly detection using deep learning algorithms.
  • Introduction of RB methods to reduce computational complexity for real-time monitoring.
  • Successful integration of IoT systems for continuous real-time monitoring.

Methodologies

I apply Agile project management techniques combined with the Eisenhower Matrix to prioritize and execute tasks efficiently.
