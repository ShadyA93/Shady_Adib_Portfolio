About Me

Dr. Shady Adib is a PhD candidate in the Civil Engineering department at the University of Newcastle, Newcastle Upon Tyne, UK. His doctoral research leverages the rapid advancement of digital technology and data-driven approaches to innovate real-time damage identification in infrastructure.

Dr. Adib's PhD research focuses on the development of the Digital Twin (DT) concept, which integrates mathematical and data-driven approaches for monitoring and managing the health of structures. By combining the Reduced Basis (RB) method and Deep Learning (DL) technology, his research aims at detecting abnormal changes in structural behaviour in real-time. This innovative approach addresses model uncertainties and synchronises physical and virtual models using Internet of Things (IoT) technology.

Before his PhD, Dr. Adib completed his master’s degree in Structural Engineering (2017-2019) at Vilnius Gediminas Technical University, Vilnius, Lithuania, focusing on structural analysis, design, and application of high-strength steel cold-formed tubular beams. He also holds a bachelor’s degree in Civil Engineering (2012-2017) from the German University in Cairo, Cairo, Egypt, where he concentrated on comparing reinforced concrete and steel structures.

Research

Research Focus: Dr. Adib's PhD research focuses on the development of the DT concept, which integrates both mathematical models and data-driven approaches for monitoring and managing the health of civil engineering structures. By combining the RB method and DL technology, his research aims to detect abnormal changes in structural behaviour in real-time, allowing for immediate interventions to prevent potential failures.

Dr. Adib's work demonstrates significant improvements in the accuracy and efficiency of structural health monitoring (SHM). His findings include:

  • Development of real-time structural anomaly detection using deep learning algorithms.
  • Introduction of RB methods to reduce the computational complexity of large-scale models, ensuring faster response times.
  • Successful integration of IoT systems for continuous, real-time monitoring of physical assets.
  • A solution for managing model uncertainties, improving the reliability of predictive maintenance strategies in civil infrastructure.

Research Interests:

  • Advanced Machine Learning Integration for SHM: Developing machine learning algorithms to enhance SHM systems, including generative AI models and physics-informed deep learning frameworks.
  • Internet of Everything (IoE) and SHM Integration: Exploring the integration of IoE with SHM, incorporating human insights with data from machines and systems for comprehensive monitoring and analysis.
  • Self-Generating Digital Twins: Developing self-generating digital twins for SHM, allowing for continuous updates and improvements based on real-time data.
  • Generative AI and Physics-Informed Models: Integrating physics-informed models to create reliable DTs and visually represent structural damage in real-time.
  • Self-Healing Materials: Investigating the potential of self-healing materials for SHM systems to autonomously repair damage.
